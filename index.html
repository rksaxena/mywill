ITS HIGH TIME TO MAKE YOUR WILL!!!

A Will or testament is a legal document by which you can decide the future of your all properties, investments, arms and ammunitions, gold, cash, locker etc. (PIAGCL) when you are no more in the world. The registration of the Will is not compulsory. You can always change your Will at your will and for any number of times. It has a very significant role in India for disposal of your properties as well as for making it easier for your successor to inherit your property without much legal hassles.

There is no age for making a Will. If you have property or any of the above mentioned investments or assets in your name and you wish to decide its future, you must make a Will. Covid-19 has made our life very unpredictable. You may be a doctor, businessman, government-retired or serving or have a secured job in a private company, it is highly recommended not to leave your hard earned assets in an uncertain future; it is highly recommended to ensure that only your descendant(s) or the people you wish, may get your assets and possessions after your life.

Who can make a Will?

RELIGION

A Hindu, Buddhist, Sikh or Jain can make his or her Will. Mohammedans are governed by personal law and they can dispose their property according to Muslim Law.

OCCUPATION

You may be a doctor, businessman, professional, government-retired or serving, an actor or have a secured job in a private company; in short, any person can make a Will.

To whom you can make a Will?

  • You can make a Will in favour of your descendants, deciding their shares in your property.
  • You can make a Will even in favour of some unknown person whom you trust, giving complete or part of your possessions.
  • You can make a Will even in favour of your grand-children or any minor for securing their future when they attain majority.
  • You must make a Will even if you have only one child.
